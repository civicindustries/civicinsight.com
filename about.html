Company

Civic Insight makes real-time government data about a community’s underutilized spaces – think abandoned homes, empty storefronts, or vacant lots – both publicly available and extremely easy to use and understand. By getting everyone on the same page, Civic Insight makes it easier for government staff, motivated citizens, and local organizations to collaborate more effectively to improve the quality and value of their neighborhoods.

Meet the team

Eddie is co-founder and CEO of Civic Insight, where he lends his experience bringing modern technologies and practices to civic institutions. Previously, Eddie was a 2012 Code for America fellow and was one of the creators of BlightStatus for the City of New Orleans.

Before Code for America, he co-founded a web consulting company and led independent, open-source projects that are currently used by universities, governments and libraries across the country. Eddie is engaged in his home city of Oakland, where he is the co-founder and Director of Technology of OpenOakland, and serves on the Public Ethics Commission for the City of Oakland overseeing government transparency.

Alex brings her expertise in design and communication to the Civic Insight team as co-founder and Chief Creative Officer, leading in sales, marketing and design activities. Alex's human-centered approach ensures that Civic Insight meet the needs of citizens and city staff in the most effective and intuitive way possible.

Before Civic Insight, Alex was a 2012 Code for America fellow partnering with the city of New Orleans to help create tools to make government more transparent, efficient, and connected to citizens. Before that, she created print and web design solutions for companies like Avon, New York Magazine, and The Future Project.

As co-founder and CTO of Civic Insight, Amir leads efforts in client on-boarding and integration, quality assurance, and technical support, to provide reliable service to agencies operating Civic Industries' products and platforms.

Before Civic Insight, Amir worked as an investment banking technologist in NYC, focused on equities trading and fixed-income business lines. With aspirations to repurpose his technology skills to better US cities, Amir served as a 2012 Code For America fellow, partnering with the city of New Orleans to develop applications focusing on vacancy and quality of life issues.

As senior front-end programmer, Chach adds new features and interface improvements to the CivicInsight app for all city integrations.

Also a Code for America Alum ('11), Chach has years of experience with innovative interface, map and data projects for the public through her work with science museums, non-profits and open-source projects. As a civic artist, she created Lemonopoly, Fruit Fence, co-founded Nerds for Nature and the Iconathon, and spent a year mapping water data for California.
